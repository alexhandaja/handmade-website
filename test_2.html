Historical Context

Chinese in Indonesia:

1. The Chinese make up less than 5% of the population, but they control about 70% of Indonesia's wealth.

2. An Islamist leader accused the President of Indonesia of favoring the ethnic Chinese. This ethnic wealth gap has played a part in the resentment that "pribumi" have for them.

3. The hostility between the Chinese and Indonesians all began during the period where we were colonized by the Dutch. They created a class system known as the Dutch East Indies which was the origin of centuries of racial division.

Europeans: First-Class

Chinese and Indians: Second-Class

Chinese and Indians: Second-Class

Indonesians: Third-Class (exploited and controlled) (they were tortured, raped, executed)

This divide caused distrust in the Chinese. Economic factors played a role: most natives were poor and perceived the Chinese as occupying some of the most prosperous neighborhoods in the city.
